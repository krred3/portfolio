Comparative Neural Network Models for Inverse Kinematics Prediction

Deep Learning architectures (FNN, RNN, LSTM) for robotic motion planning

View Project Report View Code & Dataset

Inverse Kinematics (IK), the process of calculating the precise joint angles required to position a robotic armâ€™s end-effector, is traditionally solved using iterative numerical methods like the Jacobian transpose. These methods can be computationally expensive and prone to singularities in real-time applications.

To overcome these latency bottlenecks, we developed a data-driven solution that replaces complex geometric computations with high-speed neural network predictions, effectively enabling near-instantaneous motion planning for hyper-redundant manipulators. We designed and trained three distinct deep learning architectures: Feedforward Neural Networks (FNN), Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN), and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks on a comprehensive dataset of joint space trajectories to learn the non-linear mapping between spatial coordinates and joint configurations.

The study revealed that the LSTM architecture, specifically engineered to capture sequential dependencies in motion paths, offered the optimal balance of accuracy and computational efficiency, validating the potential of "Black Box" AI models to solve deterministic mechanical control problems in dynamic, high-speed environments.
