Design and Development of Arched Beam using Vacuum Infusion

Optimized CFRP Structure for High Strength-to-Weight Ratio

View Project Report

The challenge was to design and manufacture a carbon fiber reinforced polymer (CFRP) arched beam capable of withstanding maximum compressive loads while maintaining a high strength-to-weight ratio. The competition constraints required the beam to fit within a specific volumetric envelope and sustain loading without catastrophic brittle failure.

My primary engineering contribution was the ply schedule optimization and manufacturing strategy using Vacuum Assisted Resin Infusion (VARI). Our initial design suffered from resin starvation and delamination at the vertical walls due to poor flow mesh placement. Learning from this, we engineered a Design 2 strategy featuring a continuous C-shaped ±45°/0° ply schedule. This geometry wrapped the carbon fabric from the top flange down the vertical walls to eliminate the butt-joint discontinuities that typically fail under shear.

We also optimized the resin infusion ports to prevent race-tracking and ensure full saturation of the complex curves. Under 3-point bend testing (Instron), the optimized beam withstood a peak load of 2,908 lbf (12.9 kN). More importantly, it exhibited a progressive failure mode rather than snapping instantly, which resulted in 80% higher energy absorption compared to the class average. This validated the C-shaped design strategy as a superior method for resisting shear loads in hollow composite structures.

Key Engineering Takeaways

Composite Manufacturing

Mastering Vacuum Assisted Resin Infusion (VARI) to produce void-free aerospace-grade parts.

Failure Analysis

Identifying interlaminar shear as the root cause of failure and redesigning the ply schedule to mitigate it.

Material Selection

Balancing the orthotropic properties of Carbon Fiber (0° for bending, ±45° for shear) to optimize structural performance.

Mechanical Testing

Validating simulation models against real-world Instron destructive testing data.
